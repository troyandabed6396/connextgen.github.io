About Us

As high school students, we know what it's like to want to develop our interests. However, the scarcity of accessible learning avenues for high school students outside the classroom makes bridging the gap between interests and hands-on experience more challenging.

ConnextGen is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit organization committed to providing all high school students the opportunity to explore career paths and gain practical skills working on real-world applications.

Our Process

Shadow

  • Students are matched up with an industry mentor based on their interests
  • They shadow and work under their mentor at their workspace once a week over the course of three months

Identify

  • Each student will identify a researchable real-world problem related to their shadowed workspace or another field of interest
  • If a student is having trouble coming up with ideas, they will be provided with some potential options by their mentors

Develop

  • During and between their shadowing sessions, students will research their chosen problem and develop a project addressing the issue
  • Their solution can take the form of a prototype, proposal, or comprehensive report
  • Students will present their final projects to the ConnextGen team and other mentors at the end of the program
